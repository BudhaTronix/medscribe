This synthetic reference document outlines chronic kidney disease staging for an engineering demo. It is not medical advice. For a fictional patient such as Mr Aaron Vale, the assessment starts by confirming that reduced kidney function or kidney damage is persistent rather than a single abnormal result. Estimated glomerular filtration rate is grouped into G categories, while urine albumin to creatinine ratio is grouped into A categories. Both dimensions matter because a patient with modestly reduced filtration and heavy albuminuria may have higher risk than a patient with a lower eGFR but no albuminuria. The note records blood pressure, diabetes status, cardiovascular history, medication list, smoking, family history, fluid status and prior imaging when available.

Common reversible contributors are reviewed before labelling progression. These include dehydration, recent contrast exposure, obstruction, non steroidal anti inflammatory medicines, acute infection and changes in renin angiotensin system medicines or diuretics. Baseline tests in this demo include creatinine with eGFR, electrolytes, bicarbonate if locally used, calcium, phosphate when appropriate, haemoglobin, urine albumin to creatinine ratio and urinalysis. The clinician also checks for haematuria, rapidly falling eGFR, resistant hypertension and systemic symptoms, because these may require specialist input.

Management is risk based. Blood pressure control, diabetes optimisation, smoking cessation, cardiovascular risk reduction and avoidance of nephrotoxins are central. ACE inhibitors or angiotensin receptor blockers are considered when albuminuria is present and tolerated, with potassium and creatinine monitoring after initiation or dose changes. SGLT 2 inhibitors may be considered for eligible patients according to current local policy. The patient receives a clear sick day discussion for medicines that may need temporary review during dehydration or severe illness. Red flags include rapidly declining kidney function, severe hyperkalaemia, pulmonary oedema, uraemic symptoms, nephrotic range proteinuria, visible haematuria with systemic illness and suspected obstruction. The plan states CKD category, albuminuria category, trend, medicines to avoid, monitoring interval and referral triggers.

Follow up also includes communication. The patient should understand that kidney staging is not a single score of how they feel today, but a way to estimate risk and plan monitoring. Medicine reconciliation is repeated because dose adjustment may be needed for renally cleared drugs, and over the counter anti inflammatory medicines can be overlooked. Diet advice is individualised and avoids unnecessary restriction unless there is a clear indication. The demo note records uncertainty, such as missing prior creatinine values, and sets a concrete date for repeat testing.
